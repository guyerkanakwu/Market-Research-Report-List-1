<p><p>Preserved eggs, also known as century eggs or hundred-year eggs, are a traditional Chinese delicacy made by preserving duck, chicken, or quail eggs in a mixture of clay, ash, salt, and quicklime. The preservation process imparts a distinctive flavor and a unique dark green to black color with a translucent jelly-like texture. This product has gained popularity not only in Asian cuisine but also among health-conscious consumers due to its nutritional benefits and unique taste.</p><p>The Preserved Egg Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 14.8% during the forecast period, driven by increasing consumer interest in ethnic foods and exotic flavors. As global culinary trends shift towards unique and authentic gastronomical experiences, preserved eggs are finding their way into various international cuisines. Additionally, rising awareness about the nutritional value and health benefits associated with preserved eggs, such as their high protein content and low calorie count, is further propelling market growth. Innovations in packaging and distribution, along with the growing demand for ready-to-eat food products, are also contributing to the expansion of the preserved egg market, making it a vibrant segment in the food industry.</p></p>

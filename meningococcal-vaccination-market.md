<p><p>The meningococcal vaccination market comprises three primary types: polysaccharide, conjugate, and combination vaccines. Polysaccharide vaccines contain capsular polysaccharides and induce a moderate immune response but do not provide long-term immunity. Conjugate vaccines link polysaccharides to proteins, enhancing immune response and durability, particularly in younger populations. Combination vaccines integrate meningococcal components with other vaccines, offering broader protection while simplifying immunization schedules. Together, these types address varying demographic needs and improve public health outcomes against meningococcal disease.</p></p>

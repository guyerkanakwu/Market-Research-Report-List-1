<p><p>The dental prosthetic and implant consumables market encompasses various types of implants used for tooth replacement. Endosteal implants are the most common, surgically placed into the jawbone, providing stability and support for crowns or dentures. Subperiosteal implants, on the other hand, are placed under the gum but above the jawbone, suitable for patients with insufficient bone density. Both types aim to restore function and aesthetics, catering to diverse patient needs in dental care.</p></p>

<p><p>Interference microscopes are advanced optical instruments used to enhance the contrast of transparent and semi-transparent specimens. The market consists of two primary types: double beam microscopes, which utilize two overlapping light beams to create interference patterns that aid in precise imaging, and multibeam microscopes, which employ multiple beams simultaneously for high-resolution imaging and enhanced depth-of-field. Both types are essential in material science, biology, and nanotechnology for detailed analysis of samples without staining or damaging them.</p></p>

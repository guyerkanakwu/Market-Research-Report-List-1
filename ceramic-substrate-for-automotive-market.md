<p><p>Ceramic substrates in the automotive market include several types, each with unique characteristics. Direct Bonded Copper (DBC) substrates feature a robust copper layer for excellent thermal conductivity. Active Metal Brazed (AMB) substrates provide superior mechanical strength and thermal performance. Direct Bonded Aluminum (DBA) substrates offer lightweight options with good thermal management. Direct Pressed Ceramic (DPC) substrates primarily focus on reliability. Low-Temperature Co-fired Ceramic (LTCC) and High-Temperature Co-fired Ceramic (HTCC) substrates are designed for complex circuitry and high-frequency applications, balancing performance and cost.</p></p>
